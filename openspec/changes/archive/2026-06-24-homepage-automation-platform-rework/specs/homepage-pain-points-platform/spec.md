## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Pain point 1 is module-agnostic
The first pain point card (currently "Every notice starts from a blank page") SHALL be rewritten to describe the broader problem of compliance work starting from scratch — applicable to notice replies, balance sheets, appeals, and TDS audits — not only to notice drafting.

#### Scenario: Pain point 1 resonates with non-notice visitors
- **WHEN** an accounting department head reads pain point 1
- **THEN** the heading and description SHALL describe a problem they recognise (e.g., recurring compliance tasks starting from scratch, statutory deadlines, manual repetition) without requiring them to be a notice filer

#### Scenario: Pain point 1 still captures the notice audience
- **WHEN** a CA practitioner who handles notices reads pain point 1
- **THEN** the pain point SHALL still be recognisable as describing their experience — notices MAY be mentioned as one example among others

### Requirement: Bridge paragraph references platform breadth and upcoming modules
The `problem-bridge` paragraph that follows the three pain cards SHALL reference "fourteen workflows" (or the current count) and SHALL include a mention of more modules in development (e.g., "Twenty more in development").

#### Scenario: Bridge paragraph mentions modules in development
- **WHEN** a visitor reads the paragraph following the pain cards
- **THEN** it SHALL contain a forward-looking reference to upcoming modules (e.g., "with twenty more in development" or equivalent)
