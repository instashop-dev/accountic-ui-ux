## [CA Firm Automation](/blog/ca-firm-automate-kyc-engagement-letters/): GST Notice-to-Reply Tracker in Zoho

If you manage GST compliance for more than 50 clients, you already know the feeling. A DRC-01 lands in your client's email, gets forwarded to a junior, sits in an inbox over a long weekend, and by Thursday morning the reply deadline has passed. No one person is at fault — the system itself is broken.

This article walks you through building a structured, automated GST notice tracker inside Zoho so your firm never misses a Show Cause Notice deadline again.

---

### Why GST Notice Management Breaks Down at Scale

Most firms start with a shared Excel sheet. It works for 20 clients. At 50 clients and beyond, it collapses. Notices pile up across email inboxes, WhatsApp forwards, and handwritten registers with no single source of truth.

The deeper problem is ownership. When a notice is emailed to info@yourfirm.com and forwarded to three people, nobody actually owns it. There is no accountability trail, no escalation path, and no visibility for the partner until the deadline is already breathing down everyone's neck.

Manual logging makes it worse during assessments. If a GST officer asks for proof that you replied to a DRC-01 on a specific date, a spreadsheet entry with no attached acknowledgement is nearly worthless. Audit gaps at this stage can expose both the client and your firm to unnecessary risk.

---

### Anatomy of a GST Notice Lifecycle: What Your Tracker Must Cover

Before you build anything in Zoho, map out every notice type and status your firm handles. If a field is not in the system at Day 1, your team will create workarounds that defeat the entire purpose.

The table below covers the key notice types, their typical response windows, and the mandatory fields your tracker must record for each.

| Notice Type | Typical Reply Window | Mandatory Fields to Capture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Show Cause Notice (SCN) | 30 days (extendable) | GSTIN, notice date, section, reply due date, assigned staff, current status |
| DRC-01 (demand notice) | 30 days from issue | GSTIN, demand amount, tax period, ARN of reply, payment reference if any |
| DRC-03 (voluntary payment) | Voluntary — no fixed deadline | GSTIN, payment date, challan number, linked SCN reference |
| Intimation / clarification | 7–15 days (as specified) | GSTIN, clarification sought, date of response, officer name |
| Assessment order | 3 months for appeal (if contested) | Order date, demand confirmed, appeal filed date, forum (Appellate Authority / Tribunal) |

Outcome states are equally important. Your tracker must allow the following status values: Pending, Replied, Adjournment Sought, Order Received, Rectification Filed, and Appeal Initiated. Forcing your team to pick from a defined list — rather than typing freehand — is what makes your data reportable later.

Firms often default to whatever Zoho product they already use. That instinct is understandable but not always right for a notice tracker. Here is a direct comparison.

| Zoho Product | Build Effort | Customisation Depth | Suitability for Notice Tracker |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Zoho Creator | Medium (3–5 days initial setup) | Very high — custom forms, Deluge scripting, REST API, Kanban views | Best choice for firms with 50+ clients; scales cleanly |
| Zoho CRM (Deals pipeline) | Low (1 day repurposing) | Limited — no native document-attachment workflows, pipeline stages are generic | Acceptable short-term workaround, breaks at volume |
| Zoho Projects | Low to medium | Moderate — good for task-level deadlines but lacks structured data fields for notice metadata | Not recommended; misses critical notice log fields |

The recommendation is Zoho Creator for any firm managing more than 30 active notices at any point. The initial build investment pays back within the first month of avoiding a single missed SCN deadline and the consequential demand that follows.

### Step-by-Step: Building the GST Notice Tracker in Zoho Creator

This is the core of your build. Follow these steps in sequence — skipping ahead, particularly on the Client Master lookup, creates data integrity problems that are painful to fix retroactively.

1. Create a **Client Master** form in Zoho Creator with fields for GSTIN, legal name, trade name, registration state, and assigned partner. This becomes the lookup source for every notice record so GSTIN populates automatically when your team selects a client name.

2. Create the **Notice Log** form and add a lookup field pointing to Client Master. Include all mandatory fields from the table above: notice type (dropdown), notice date, reply due date, assigned CA/staff (user lookup), current status (dropdown with defined values), demand amount (currency field), and a long-text field for remarks.

3. Add a **Document Attachment** sub-form to the Notice Log that accepts uploads for the original notice PDF, draft reply, filed reply with acknowledgement, and any supporting annexures. Naming the attachment type via a dropdown keeps retrieval clean.

4. Write a **Deluge workflow** triggered on form submission and on daily schedule. The workflow checks if (Reply Due Date − Today) equals 7 or equals 1, and if Status is still Pending. On match, it sends an email to the assigned staff member and posts a Cliq message to the firm's GST notices channel with the client name, notice type, and days remaining.

5. Create a **Kanban report view** inside Zoho Creator with columns mapped to: Pending → Replied → Order Received → Closed. Set the card title to Client Name, subtitle to Notice Type, and colour-code by days remaining (red for less than 3 days, amber for 3–7 days, green for 7+). This becomes your team lead's daily triage dashboard.

6. Publish the application to your internal team with role-based access: junior staff can create and update records; managers can change status to Closed; partners have read access to all records plus delete rights. Never give bulk-delete access to junior roles.

7. Run a **pilot for two weeks** with one partner's client portfolio before rolling out firm-wide. Use this window to catch any missing dropdown values, adjust the Deluge date logic for GST portal holidays, and train your team on the mandatory fields before bad data habits form.

### Automating Reminders and Escalations With Zoho Flow

Once Zoho Creator is live, connect it to your communication channels using Zoho Flow. This moves reminders from optional to automatic, which is the only standard that works at scale.

Set up a Flow trigger that fires daily and checks the Notice Log for records where (Reply Due Date − Today) equals 7. The Flow sends a direct Cliq message to the assigned staff member and, if your firm uses WhatsApp Business via an approved provider connected to Zoho Flow, a WhatsApp nudge to the client's CA team contact. This 7-day window is your comfortable buffer for drafting and partner review.

The escalation logic is the part most firms skip and later regret. Build a second Flow that checks for records where Status is still Pending and Reply Due Date is 48 hours away. This Flow sends an email directly to the partner-in-charge with a summary of every such notice, flagging client name, GSTIN, notice type, and hours remaining. The partner can then intervene immediately rather than finding out on the morning of the deadline.

Every automated reminder — both the 7-day ping and the 48-hour escalation — should be logged as a child record in the Notice Log under a **Communication Trail** sub-form. This creates an immutable audit log showing when reminders were sent and to whom, which is invaluable if a staff member later claims they were never notified.

**Client Scenario:**

A three-partner CA firm in Pune manages approximately 120 active GST clients across sectors including manufacturing, e-commerce, and professional services. Before the Zoho tracker, their notice management ran on a shared Google Sheet with colour-coded rows. On two separate occasions in the prior financial year, SCN reply deadlines were missed because the assigned junior had resigned mid-month and handover was incomplete.

After deploying Zoho Creator using the workflow above, the firm segmented notices by partner ownership using the lookup field. Each partner now has a personalised dashboard showing only their portfolio's notices — a critical feature when three partners have very different client risk profiles and preferred working styles.

The firm's managing partner set up a **weekly review report** that filters all notices with a reply due date within the next 14 days. This report is auto-emailed to all three partners every Monday morning at 8:30 AM via a scheduled Zoho Creator report delivery. Monday morning's first 15 minutes is now a structured triage meeting rather than a panicked inbox search.

After six months of live operation, the firm's office manager runs a monthly analytics chart from Zoho Creator's built-in reporting. The chart plots notice resolution rate by notice type — SCN, DRC-01, assessment orders — and shows average days from receipt to reply closure. In the first quarter after go-live, their average reply turnaround dropped from 19 days to 11 days, and zero deadlines were missed across the 120-client portfolio.

### Scaling the Tracker: Integrations and Enhancements for Growing Firms

Once the base tracker is stable and your team is using it consistently, the next phase is eliminating manual data entry entirely. The biggest time sink at this stage is your staff manually typing notice details from the GST portal into Zoho Creator.

Use Zoho Creator's REST API to accept a JSON payload from a GST portal scraping tool or a third-party compliance platform such as ClearTax, Masters India, or your GSTP software. The API endpoint receives notice metadata — GSTIN, notice type, date, reference number — and creates the Notice Log record automatically, leaving your team only to assign ownership and confirm the reply due date. This single integration can save 30–45 minutes per day in a high-volume firm.

For hearings and adjudication, link reply documents stored in Zoho WorkDrive directly to the notice record using WorkDrive's folder URL or the Zoho Creator–WorkDrive native integration. When your firm appears before the Additional Commissioner or the Appellate Authority, every document for that notice is retrievable from a single record with one click — no hunting through email or asking juniors to locate files on a laptop.

When a notice is marked Closed in the tracker, trigger a Zoho Flow that either raises a Zoho Books invoice for the compliance service fee or creates a Zoho CRM deal to signal billing. This closes the commercial loop and ensures that notice work — which often goes unbilled because it is handled as part of a retainer — is at minimum logged as a completed service unit for internal productivity tracking.
