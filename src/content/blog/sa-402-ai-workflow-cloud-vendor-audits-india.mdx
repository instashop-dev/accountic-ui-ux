## SA 402 AI Workflow for Cloud Vendor Audits in India

---

### SA 402 and Cloud Vendors: Why Indian Auditors Must Act Now

If your audit client runs payroll on GreytHR, books invoices on Zoho Books, or processes payments through RazorpayX, you already have a live SA 402 exposure — whether you have documented it or not. SA 402, *Audit Considerations Relating to an Entity Using a Service Organisation*, requires you as the user entity auditor to assess controls at third-party platforms that are relevant to the financial statements. Most Indian audit files I review have a blank or boilerplate paragraph on this. That is a quality management gap.

SA 402 mirrors ISA 402 closely but sits squarely within ICAI's Indian audit framework and applies to every statutory audit where a cloud vendor processes, stores, or transmits data that flows into the books. The standard has existed for years, but the trigger point for Indian auditors arrived with the explosive SME and mid-market adoption of SaaS-based accounting, ERP, and payroll tools between 2020 and 2024.

Regulators are now paying attention. SEBI's cybersecurity circular for listed entities, RBI's IT governance guidelines for regulated entities, and MCA's emerging data governance expectations all directly scrutinise controls at third-party SaaS vendors. If a regulator asks your listed audit client why their ERP vendor's access controls were never reviewed, the audit file is the first thing they will look at.

### Key SA 402 Concepts Every Indian Auditor Must Understand

Before you build an AI-assisted workflow, you need the conceptual architecture right. The standard draws clear lines between who is responsible for what, and mixing up these roles creates audit file disasters.

The **user entity auditor** — that is you, the statutory auditor of the client — is responsible for obtaining sufficient appropriate audit evidence about controls at the service organisation. The **service auditor** is an independent auditor engaged directly by the service organisation to issue a report on its controls. You rely on the service auditor's report but remain responsible for your own conclusion.

When evaluating a SOC report from an Indian or global cloud vendor, two reporting methods apply to subservice organisations:

- **Carve-out method**: The service organisation excludes subservice organisation controls from its description and report. You must perform additional procedures for those carved-out controls.
- **Inclusive method**: Subservice organisation controls are included in the service organisation's description and covered by the service auditor's report. Less additional work required for you.

| Concept | Type 1 SOC / ISAE 3402 Report | Type 2 SOC / ISAE 3402 Report |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What it covers | Design and implementation of controls at a point in time | Design, implementation, and operating effectiveness over a period |
| Sufficiency for SA 402 | Limited — confirms controls exist but not that they worked | Generally sufficient if period covers your audit window |
| When you need more | Always supplement with CUECs and direct inquiry | Only when period gap exists or qualifications are present |
| Indian vendor availability | Common for newer SaaS vendors (Zoho, Razorpay) | Less common; large global vendors (SAP, Oracle) usually have Type 2 |
| Action if unavailable | Issue vendor questionnaire and document alternative procedures | Escalate to engagement partner and consider impact on audit opinion |

The critical practical point: a Type 1 report tells you the control was designed correctly on a single day. It tells you nothing about whether it operated for the twelve months your client's financial year covers. Never treat a Type 1 report as full SA 402 compliance without supplementary procedures.

### Mapping Indian Cloud Vendors to SA 402 Risk Categories

Not every cloud tool your client uses creates equal audit exposure. A disciplined risk-tiering approach lets you allocate your AI-assisted workflow effort where it matters most.

**Client Scenario:**

Consider a mid-sized Mumbai-based manufacturer with a ₹800 crore turnover. Their technology stack includes GreytHR for payroll (3,200 employees), SAP Business One Cloud for ERP and financial reporting, ClearTax GST for e-invoicing and return filing, and Microsoft 365 for email and document storage. Here is how you tier these under SA 402:

**High-Risk Tier** includes platforms that directly generate or process financial statement data. GreytHR processes the entire salary, PF, ESIC, and TDS computation, which flows directly into P&L and balance sheet. SAP Business One Cloud hosts the general ledger, fixed assets, and financial close. A control failure at either platform could materially misstate the financial statements. These require full SA 402 documentation, SOC report evaluation, and complementary user entity control (CUEC) testing.

**Medium-Risk Tier** covers platforms that process transaction-level data with indirect financial statement impact. ClearTax GST handles e-invoicing data and GSTR filing, which carries GST liability and input tax credit (ITC) implications. Karza Technologies, if used for KYC and vendor verification, processes data that supports transaction authenticity. These require SOC report review or vendor questionnaire plus specific CUECs around data reconciliation.

**Low-Risk Tier** includes tools where the financial statement link is indirect. Microsoft 365 stores audit trails and emails but does not compute financial data. Google Workspace similarly provides communication infrastructure. These typically need only a brief risk assessment note confirming limited SA 402 exposure, with a focus on data security incident monitoring.

This three-tier model is your first prompt input when you deploy AI tools in the workflow below.

### AI-Assisted SA 402 Audit Workflow for Cloud Vendors

Here is the complete workflow your team should follow. Each step is executable with current AI tools available to Indian CA firms today.

1. Extract the client's full IT asset register from the engagement intake questionnaire and import it into your AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or a firm-licensed LLM). Prompt the model to identify all vendors that process, store, or transmit financial data, categorising them by the data type handled — payroll, GL, invoicing, payments, or reporting.

2. Cross-reference the AI-generated vendor list against your firm's cloud vendor registry (see firm strategy section below). Flag any vendor that appears in the client's stack but has not been pre-assessed at the firm level. These require fresh SA 402 scoping.

3. Score each identified vendor using a structured risk prompt. Feed the AI the vendor name, data processed, number of transactions, and regulatory context (listed entity, RBI-regulated, etc.). Request a risk tier output with a brief rationale. Review the output — AI risk scoring is a starting point, not a final decision. Partner sign-off is required for all High-Risk tier conclusions.

4. Request SOC 1 Type 2 or SOC 2 Type 2 reports from each High-Risk and Medium-Risk vendor through a formal letter. For Indian vendors without SOC reports, issue a structured vendor control questionnaire covering access management, change management, incident response, and data backup.

5. Upload the SOC report PDF to your AI tool and prompt it to extract: report period, auditor name and independence statement, control objectives, exceptions noted, and qualified or adverse findings. Verify the AI extraction manually against the first three pages and the exceptions section — LLMs occasionally miss footnotes.

6. Map identified SOC controls against your client's financial statement assertions using an AI-assisted control matrix. Prompt the model: *"For each control in this SOC report, identify which financial statement assertion (existence, completeness, accuracy, cut-off, rights and obligations) it addresses for [client name]'s [payroll/GL/invoicing] process."* Review the mapping output and adjust for client-specific risks.

7. Identify control gaps — controls present in the SOC report that do not cover your full audit period, carved-out subservice organisations, or control objectives not addressed. For each gap, document the alternative procedure you will perform and assign it to a team member with a completion date.

8. Use AI to draft the SA 402 working paper narrative. Provide the model with your risk tier, SOC report summary, gap list, and CUEC testing results. Prompt it to produce a working paper section in the format required by your firm's audit management platform. Review and edit — never file AI-drafted narrative without partner review.

9. Draft the management representation letter clause covering SA 402. The clause must confirm that management has reviewed and is responsible for implementing all CUECs identified in the SOC report, that no material incidents occurred at service organisations during the period, and that all vendor SLA breaches were reported to the auditor. AI can draft this clause; you must confirm the facts with management.

10. Assemble the final SA 402 working paper file: vendor risk register, SOC report extracts with your annotations, gap analysis, alternative procedure results, CUEC testing evidence, and management representation. Cross-reference every document to your audit programme step. The file must be self-explanatory to a reviewer who has not attended a single client meeting.

### Evaluating SOC Reports and Bridging Indian Compliance Gaps

Receiving a SOC report is the start of the work, not the end. Three immediate questions matter: Is the report current? Does it cover your audit period? Are there qualified opinions or exceptions?

On **report currency**, a SOC 2 Type 2 report issued 18 months before your audit date is a red flag. Industry practice treats a report as current if it covers a period ending no more than 12 months before your audit report date. If the gap is larger, request a bridge letter from the service organisation's auditor or perform additional direct procedures.

On **period alignment**, your audit is for the financial year ended 31 March. If the SOC report covers 1 July to 30 June, there is a nine-month overlap but a three-month gap in Q1 of your audit year. Document that gap explicitly, note what alternative procedures cover it (client-side access logs, incident register, management inquiry), and evidence those procedures on file.

On **qualified opinions or exceptions**, never bury these in the working paper. A single exception in change management controls at a payroll processor could mean unauthorised code changes affected salary computations. Evaluate each exception against the specific financial statement assertion it threatens, document your conclusion, and escalate to the engagement partner if materiality is in question.

**Handling vendors with no SOC report** is increasingly common with Indian SaaS vendors. Issue a formal vendor inquiry letter on your firm's letterhead requesting responses to a structured questionnaire. The questionnaire should cover logical access controls, segregation of duties, backup and recovery, change management, and incident response. Supplement this with a review of the vendor's publicly available security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2 in progress, CERT-In compliance). Document that you considered the sufficiency of this evidence and concluded it was adequate — or explain why you escalated the matter.

For **Indian-specific compliance gaps**, three areas require explicit attention in every SA 402 file today. First, the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDP Act) creates data localisation and processing obligations. If a cloud vendor processes personal data of Indian residents on servers outside India without DPDP-compliant safeguards, that is an audit risk. Second, GST data integrity at e-invoicing platforms affects the accuracy of ITC claims and output tax liability in the financial statements — these are directly material. Third, RBI's cybersecurity guidelines for regulated entities (banks, NBFCs) require specific controls at technology service providers. If your client is an RBI-regulated entity, your SA 402 documentation must address these requirements explicitly.

### Complementary User Entity Controls: Documentation Checklist

Even the most comprehensive SOC Type 2 report assumes the client is doing its part. CUECs are controls that the service organisation's auditor has identified as necessary for the client to maintain for the overall control environment to work. Documenting these is one of the most commonly missed steps in Indian SA 402 files.

Use the following table to structure your CUEC documentation and testing plan:

| Control Category | Specific CUEC Required | Evidence to Obtain | AI Documentation Tip |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Access management | MFA enforced for all users on cloud platforms | Screenshot of MFA policy settings + user access log | Prompt AI to compare active user list against HR offboarding records to flag orphaned accounts |
| Access management | Quarterly user access review by IT/finance head | Signed review sign-off sheets for the audit year | Use AI to extract review dates and flag periods where review was missed |
| Data validation | Monthly reconciliation of payroll register against GL entries | Signed reconciliation workpapers for each month | Prompt AI to check reconciliation frequency and flag months with unresolved variances |
| Data validation | E-invoice data reconciliation against GST returns | GSTR-1 vs. sales register comparison for each quarter | AI can cross-check figures extracted from GSTR portal downloads against the client's ERP export |
| Incident response | Log of service disruptions and vendor SLA breaches | Incident register maintained by client IT team | Use AI to scan email threads for vendor downtime notifications not captured in the formal log |
| Incident response | Escalation procedure for data breaches at vendor | Written escalation policy + evidence of communication | Prompt AI to confirm the policy references current CERT-In 6-hour breach reporting timelines |

When testing CUECs, avoid the common mistake of simply asking management "do you do this?" That is an inquiry, not evidence. Obtain the actual access logs, reconciliation workpapers, or incident registers and examine them. AI tools can accelerate this by parsing large log files or spreadsheets and flagging anomalies faster than a manual review.

### Building a Scalable SA 402 Practice with AI: Firm-Level Strategy

Individual engagement-level diligence is necessary but not sufficient. If every audit team starts from scratch on SA 402 each year, you are wasting resources and creating inconsistency across your firm's quality management system. The answer is institutionalisation.

**Build a firm-wide cloud vendor registry.** This is a centralised database of every cloud vendor encountered across your client portfolio, rated by SA 402 risk tier, with the most recent SOC report status, report period, and any exceptions noted. Update it at least annually. AI web-scraping tools can monitor vendor websites and trust portals (like Zoho's Trust Centre or AWS Artifact) for new SOC reports and flag updates to your quality management team. The initial build takes time; the ongoing maintenance with AI assistance takes hours per year.

**Train your teams on prompt engineering for SOC report work.** A well-trained senior associate should be able to upload a SOC 2 Type 2 report and return a structured gap analysis within 30 minutes using a firm-approved prompt library. The critical training point is client confidentiality: never upload client financial data, client-identifiable vendor lists, or management representation letters to public AI tools. Use only the vendor's own public SOC report — which is the service organisation's document, not your client's — in public LLMs. For anything containing client data, use a firm-licensed private LLM deployment.

**Align your SA 402 AI workflow with SQM 1.** ICAI's Standards on Quality Management 1 require firms to establish policies and procedures for engagement quality. Your AI-assisted SA 402 workflow must have documented prompts, review checkpoints, and partner sign-off requirements built in. The workflow is a quality control process, not a shortcut. Document in your firm's quality manual that AI outputs are inputs to auditor judgment, not substitutes for it. This protects you in a peer review or NFRA inspection.

Integrating SA 402 workflows into platforms like Caseware, TeamMate, or even a structured SharePoint-based audit file system ensures the working papers land in the right place, cross-referenced correctly, and accessible for EQCR (Engagement Quality Control Review) without manual assembly.

### Your Concrete Next Step

This week, pull up the engagement file for your largest active statutory audit. Open the IT systems section of the permanent file or the planning memorandum. Count the number of cloud vendors your client uses that process payroll, ledger, invoicing, or payment data. Now check whether your file contains a documented SA 402 risk assessment, a SOC report or alternative procedure evidence, and a CUEC testing record for each of those vendors.

If any of those three elements is missing for any High-Risk or Medium-Risk vendor, you have a working paper gap. Use the ten-step workflow above to close it before your audit report is signed. Start with the highest-risk vendor first — typically the payroll processor or the ERP platform — and build from there.

SA 402 documentation is no longer a soft quality suggestion. With NFRA's inspection focus on working paper completeness and regulators scrutinising technology governance at listed and regulated entities, it is a hard requirement. The AI tools to do this efficiently are available now. The question is whether your firm is using them systematically or leaving the risk exposure on the table.
