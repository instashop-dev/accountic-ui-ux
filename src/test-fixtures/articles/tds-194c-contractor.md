## What is TDS Under Section 194C?

Section 194C of the Income Tax Act 1961 requires any person responsible for paying a contractor or sub-contractor to deduct tax at source (TDS) on the payment. This applies to contracts for carrying out work, including advertising, broadcasting, catering, and transportation contracts.

The deductor must have a valid TAN (Tax Deduction Account Number) to remit TDS. Example TAN format: MUMC12345E. The deductee contractor must provide their PAN (format: ABCDE1234F) to qualify for the lower TDS rate.

## TDS Rate Chart Under Section 194C

1. Individual or Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) contractors: 1% TDS
2. Company, firm, LLP, or co-operative society contractors: 2% TDS
3. Deductee without PAN or invalid PAN: 20% TDS under Section 206AA

| Contractor Type | TDS Rate | Section Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Individual / HUF | 1% | Section 194C(1) |
| Company / Firm / LLP | 2% | Section 194C(1) |
| No PAN furnished | 20% | Section 206AA |
| Transport contractor (own vehicles) | 0% with declaration | Section 194C(6) |

## Threshold Limits for TDS Deduction

TDS under Section 194C is triggered when:

- Single payment to the contractor exceeds ₹30,000, OR
- Aggregate payments to the same contractor during the financial year exceed ₹1,00,000

If neither threshold is crossed, no TDS deduction is required. However, once the aggregate limit of ₹1,00,000 is crossed mid-year, TDS applies to all subsequent payments including the payment that crossed the threshold.

**Example:** M/s ABC Constructions paid a sub-contractor three instalments: ₹28,000 in April, ₹32,000 in June, and ₹45,000 in August of FY 2024-25. The single-payment threshold was crossed in June (₹32,000 > ₹30,000), so TDS at 2% (company contractor) applies from the June payment. The August payment also attracts TDS at 2%.

## When to Deduct TDS — Payment or Credit, Whichever is Earlier

Under Section 194C, TDS must be deducted at the time of:

1. Credit of the sum to the account of the contractor, OR
2. Payment of the sum in cash, cheque, or draft

Whichever event occurs first triggers the TDS obligation. This means if a CA firm credits a contractor invoice to a provisional account before payment, TDS must be deducted at the time of crediting.

## Filing TDS Returns — Form 26Q

TDS deducted under Section 194C must be reported in Form 26Q (quarterly TDS statement for payments to residents). The due dates for filing 26Q are:

- Q1 (April–June): 31 July
- Q2 (July–September): 31 October
- Q3 (October–December): 31 January
- Q4 (January–March): 31 May

Late filing attracts a fee of ₹200 per day under Section 234E, subject to a maximum of the TDS amount.

## Common Section 194C Compliance Errors

1. Treating all payments to contractors as exempt — verify the aggregate ₹1,00,000 limit quarterly
2. Deducting TDS at 1% for company contractors — the correct rate is 2% under Section 194C(1)
3. Missing TDS on advance payments — credit to contractor's account is enough to trigger obligation
4. Not obtaining contractor's PAN at onboarding — risk of 20% TDS under Section 206AA

**CA Firm Scenario:** A firm in Chennai (TAN: CHEC98765G) engaged a transport company for office logistics at ₹15,000/month. Since the annual aggregate would reach ₹1,80,000 (exceeding ₹1,00,000), TDS at 2% applies from the first payment of the year, not just after crossing the threshold mid-year.
