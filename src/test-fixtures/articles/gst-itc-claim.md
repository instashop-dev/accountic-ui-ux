## Understanding Input Tax Credit Under GST

Input Tax Credit (ITC) allows a registered GSTIN holder to set off the CGST, SGST, and IGST paid on inward supplies against the output tax liability. Section 16 of the CGST Act lays down the eligibility conditions for claiming ITC.

A taxpayer with GSTIN 27ABCDE1234F1Z5 operating in Maharashtra can claim ITC on business purchases only if the conditions under Section 16(2) are satisfied: the supplier has filed GSTR-1, the tax has been paid to the government, and the recipient has received the goods or services.

## Eligibility Conditions Under Section 16(2)

1. The taxpayer must be registered under GST and hold a valid GSTIN
2. The goods or services must be used in the course or furtherance of business
3. The supplier must have deposited CGST, SGST, or IGST with the government
4. The tax invoice must match entries in GSTR-2B or GSTR-2A

## ITC Restrictions Under Section 17(5)

Section 17(5) of the CGST Act blocks ITC on certain categories:

| Category | ITC Blocked | Exception |
|---|---|---|
| Motor vehicles (≤13 passengers) | Yes | Transport business, driving schools |
| Food and beverages | Yes | Catering business |
| Health insurance | Yes | Statutory obligation |
| Works contract for immovable property | Yes | Further supply of works contract |
| Personal consumption | Yes | None |

## ITC Reversal Under Section 17(2) and Rule 43

When capital goods or inputs are used for both taxable and exempt supplies, ITC must be reversed proportionately. Under Rule 43, the formula is:

ITC to Reverse = (Common ITC × Value of Exempt Supplies) / Total Turnover

Example: A CA firm with aggregate turnover of ₹50,00,000 (₹40,00,000 taxable, ₹10,00,000 exempt) that claimed CGST of ₹2,00,000 on office renovation must reverse ₹40,000 (20% of ₹2,00,000).

The reversal must be reported in GSTR-3B under Table 4(B)(2) and the corresponding amount is added back to output tax liability.

## Common ITC Claim Errors and How to Avoid Them

1. Claiming ITC without matching GSTR-2B entries — always reconcile before filing GSTR-3B
2. Claiming ITC on blocked categories under Section 17(5) — maintain a blocked-credit register
3. Missing the Section 16(4) time limit — set calendar alerts for September GSTR-3B filing
4. Claiming ITC on non-business expenses — segregate personal and business invoices

## FAQ

### Can ITC be claimed on purchases made before GST registration?

No. ITC can be claimed on stock held on the date of registration, but only if the goods are inputs or capital goods and not finished goods. This is governed by Section 18(1) of the CGST Act. The claim must be filed within 30 days of obtaining registration in Form GST ITC-01.

### What happens if a supplier does not file GSTR-1?

If the supplier fails to file GSTR-1, the invoice will not appear in the recipient's GSTR-2B. Under the current GST framework, ITC is denied if the supplier has not paid tax and filed returns. The recipient must follow up with the supplier or risk losing the credit.

### How is IGST ITC utilised against CGST and SGST liability?

Under Section 49 of the CGST Act, IGST credit must first be utilised against IGST liability, then against CGST liability, and finally against SGST/UTGST liability. CGST credit cannot be used against SGST liability and vice versa.
