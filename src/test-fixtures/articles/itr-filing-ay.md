## Key Deadlines for ITR Filing AY 2024-25

Under Section 139(1) of the Income Tax Act, the due date for filing Income Tax Returns (ITR) for Assessment Year 2024-25 (FY 2023-24) is 31 July 2024 for individuals and HUFs not subject to tax audit, and 31 October 2024 for taxpayers requiring a tax audit under Section 44AB.

1. Section 139(1) — Original ITR: 31 July 2024 (non-audit) / 31 October 2024 (audit)
2. Section 139(4) — Belated return: up to 31 December 2024 with late fee
3. Section 139(5) — Revised return: up to 31 December 2024 for corrections

## Penalties for Late Filing Under Section 234F

Late filing of ITR for AY 2024-25 attracts a mandatory fee under Section 234F:

| Total Income | Late Fee (after due date, before 31 Dec 2024) |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹5,00,000 | ₹1,000 |
| Above ₹5,00,000 | ₹5,000 |
| Nil income or exempt income only | Nil |

The Section 234F fee is in addition to interest under Section 234A (1% per month on unpaid tax) which applies if taxes are not paid by the due date.

## Interest Provisions — Section 234A, 234B, and 234C

Three interest provisions commonly apply to AY 2024-25 filings:

- **Section 234A**: Interest at 1% per month for delay in filing ITR beyond due date
- **Section 234B**: Interest at 1% per month for shortfall in advance tax payment (applies if advance tax paid is less than 90% of assessed tax)
- **Section 234C**: Interest for deferment of advance tax instalments (June, September, December, March deadlines)

## Handling a Notice u/s 143(1) for AY 2024-25

Section 143(1) is an intimation (not a scrutiny notice) issued by the Centralised Processing Centre (CPC) after automated processing of the ITR. It typically flags:

1. Arithmetic errors in the return
2. Disallowance of deductions claimed without supporting computation
3. Mismatch between income reported and Form 26AS/AIS/TIS data

**Practitioner Case Study:** A Bengaluru-based salaried taxpayer (PAN: PQRST5678L, AY 2024-25) received a notice u/s 143(1) showing a demand of ₹38,500. The CPC had disallowed HRA exemption claimed u/s 10(13A) because Form 12BB was not submitted to the employer before payroll processing. The CA filed a rectification request under Section 154 with Form 16 and rent receipts as evidence. The demand was reduced to nil within 45 days of rectification filing.

## What to Check Before Filing ITR for AY 2024-25

1. Download Form 26AS, AIS, and TIS from the Income Tax portal and reconcile TDS/TCS credits
2. Verify all advance tax challans (Challan 280) against Form 26AS
3. Check that all capital gains (Section 112A, Section 111A) are correctly segregated
4. Ensure Section 80C, 80D, 80G deductions are within the prescribed limits
5. For partnerships and LLPs, verify partner remuneration limits under Section 40(b)

## New Tax Regime vs Old Tax Regime for AY 2024-25

For AY 2024-25, the new tax regime under Section 115BAC is the default regime. Taxpayers with business income must file Form 10-IEA to opt for the old regime. The deadline for filing Form 10-IEA is the same as the due date for filing ITR under Section 139(1).

**CA Practitioner Note:** For AY 2024-25, verify whether each client's aggregate deductions under Chapter VI-A (80C, 80D, 80G etc.) exceed ₹1,50,000. If total deductions are below this threshold and the client has no significant HRA or home loan interest, the new regime almost always results in lower tax liability.
